The shared frequency-domain backbone

Before the individual blocks, note that every noise source on this page is shaped by one of two closed-loop transfer functions. With open-loop gain $G_\text{ol}(s)$, the canonical type-II linear PLL model textbook (Gardner / Razavi) derived gives:

$$ H_\text{ref}(s)=\frac{\Phi_\text{out}}{\Phi_\text{ref}} = \frac{N\,G_\text{ol}(s)}{1+G_\text{ol}(s)}\ \text{(low-pass, DC}\to N)\qquad H_\text{vco}(s)=\frac{\Phi_\text{out}}{\Phi_\text{vco}} = \frac{1}{1+G_\text{ol}(s)}\ \text{(high-pass, HF}\to 1) $$

Anything entering through the reference path (REF, DTC, SPD, GM, MMD input-referred) is low-pass filtered by $H_\text{ref}$; the VCO is high-pass filtered by $H_\text{vco}$. The crossover sits at the loop bandwidth $f_c$, where the two curves cross ÔÇö that is the knob that trades reference-domain noise against VCO noise.

(1) DTC ÔÇö RC variable-slope digital-to-time converter

1. Digital-to-Time Converter (RC variable-slope)

Purpose

The DTC sits in the reference path and adds a programmable delay $\Delta t[n]$ to each CKREF edge so that, after lock, the sampling PD sees almost zero phase error ÔÇö as if the loop were integer-N slide p9. In a fractional-N PLL the divider edge CKFB wanders by up to $\pm(\text{order})\cdot T_\text{vco}/2$ relative to CKREF because the divide value toggles; the DTC predicts that wander (it is just the accumulated DSM quantization error $\Phi_\text{QE}[n]$) and delays CKREF by exactly that much, cancelling it. This is what lets the PD be high-gain and small-range ÔåÆ low in-band noise, better linearity, lower power slide p9.

Inputs / outputs

  • In: CKREF edge (here CKREF├ù2 ÔåÆ CKREFX2, slide 39); an $n$-bit delay code (main term $K_\text{DTC}\cdot\Phi_\text{QE}[n]$, plus vco_dcc and offset corrections, slide 40).
  • Out: CKDTC = CKREF delayed by $\Delta t[n]$, feeding the sampling PD.

Time-domain model

A buffer drives a resistor $R$ into a programmable LSB capacitor bank $C_\text{LSB}$, producing a voltage ramp $V_\text{dly}(t)$ that a comparator trips at $V_\text{DD}/2$. The exponential RC ramp crosses the threshold after one "$\ln 2 \cdot RC$" time constant, so the unit delay is slide p15:

$$ T_\text{res} = \ln 2 \cdot R\, C_\text{LSB} $$

The realized delay for code $D$ is $\Delta t = D\cdot T_\text{res}$ (staircase). The cancellation relation is $\Delta t[n] = K_\text{DTC}^{-1}\,\Phi_\text{QE}[n]$, with the gain $K_\text{DTC}=T_\text{vco}/\Delta t_\text{DTC}$ expressed in codes per VCO period slide p9.

Frequency-domain model

The DTC's two white noise floors enter at CKREF and are therefore shaped by $H_\text{ref}$ (low-pass ÔåÆ in-band). Quantization noise (input-referred phase PSD at CKREF) slide p15:

$$ \Phi_\text{DTC,QN} = \frac{(2\pi\,T_\text{res})^2}{12}\cdot f_\text{ref} $$
Why this form. A uniform time quantizer of step $T_\text{res}$ has timing variance $\sigma_t^2=T_\text{res}^2/12$ textbook (Bennett quantization). Convert to reference phase with $\varphi = 2\pi f_\text{ref}\,\Delta t \Rightarrow \sigma_\varphi^2=(2\pi f_\text{ref}T_\text{res})^2/12$, then spread that variance over the Nyquist span $f_\text{ref}$ to get a flat PSD ÔÇö which is exactly the slide form $\sigma_\varphi^2/f_\text{ref}$ derived. Numeric check: $T_\text{res}=400$ fs, $f_\text{ref}=104$ MHz ÔçÆ $(2\pi\cdot400\text{ fs})^2/12\cdot104\text{ MHz}=5.47\times10^{-17}\to -162.6$ dBc/Hz, i.e. the ÔêÆ163 dBc/Hz the slide quotes slide p15 derived.
The single-sided vs double-sided choice is a factor-2 (┬▒3 dB) convention. Dividing the variance by $f_\text{ref}$ (full Nyquist span) reproduces ÔêÆ163 dBc/Hz; dividing by $f_\text{ref}/2$ would read 3 dB higher needs citation (exact convention). We adopt the slide's convention everywhere.

Thermal noise floor (delay stage + INV buffer), with slew rate $k_\text{slew}=1/(2RC)$ at $V_\text{DD}/2$ and folded voltage noise $S_v^\text{folded}(f_m)\cong (kT/C)/(f_\text{out}/2)$ slide p16:

$$ \mathcal{L}\cong 10\log_{10}\!\Big[\tfrac{1}{2}\Big(\tfrac{2\pi f_\text{ref}}{k_\text{slew}}\Big)^{2} S_v^\text{folded}(f_m)\Big] \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathcal{L}\cong 10\log_{10}\!\Big[\,2kT\,f_\text{ref}\,\Big(\tfrac{2\pi}{\ln 2}\Big)^{2}\frac{2^{n}T_\text{res}^{2}}{C_\text{LSB}}\Big] $$

Physically the slew rate converts the sampled $kT/C$ voltage noise into timing jitter $\sigma_t=\sigma_v/k_\text{slew}$, then $(2\pi f_\text{ref})^2$ converts timing to reference phase. Larger $C_\text{LSB}$ ÔçÆ less $kT/C$ ÔçÆ lower noise. A floor below the PN target of ÔêÆ171 dBc/Hz needs $C_\text{LSB}\ge 2$ fF slide p16.

Dynamic range and the range/noise/power tradeoff

$$ \text{DR}=2^{n}\cdot t_\text{res}=2^{n}\ln 2\,R\,C_\text{LSB},\qquad \mathcal{L}\cong 10\log_{10}\!\Big[2kT f_\text{ref}\tfrac{(2\pi)^2}{\ln 2}\,\text{DR}\cdot R\Big],\qquad \text{QN}\propto t_\text{res}^2,\quad P_\text{DTC}\propto \text{DR}^2 $$

A 10-bit DTC ($n=10$) with $t_\text{res}=400$ fs gives $\text{DR}=400$ ps, enough to cover a 2nd-order DSM at $f_\text{vco}>5$ GHz slide p15. Because noise rises with DR and power as DR$^2$ slide p33, shrinking the range is a free win on every axis ÔÇö which is the entire motivation for the ┬¢-range (slide 34) and 1/8-range (slide 38) techniques covered with the divider below.

Main non-idealities ÔåÆ spur mechanism

  • Static INL (parabolic): code/slope-dependent comparator trip time bends the staircase. A fixed-cap array gives <2 LSB INL; DEM brings DNL to ┬▒0.2 LSB at 10-bit slide p17. The INL repeats with the DSM's periodic $\Phi_\text{QE}[n]$, so it folds into discrete fractional spurs.
  • Dynamic INL (memory): per-cycle supply settling makes the delay depend on the previous code ÔÇö a memory effect, measured at ┬▒0.6 LSB scatter slide p18. Hard to model exactly; mitigated by a master-slave regulator + bleeding current and a code reset to "1" each cycle slide p19.

(2) Sampling Phase Detector (SPD)

2. Sampling PD (slope-generator + sample/hold)

Purpose

Replaces the PFD + charge-pump with a sample-and-hold on a voltage slope, giving a much higher phase-detector gain $K_\text{SPD}$ so that downstream GM/comparator noise is suppressed and the in-band floor drops below ÔêÆ170 dBc/Hz slide p20.

Inputs / outputs

  • In: CKDTC (delayed reference edge) gates a slope generator; CKFB sets the sample instant.
  • Out: a held voltage $V_\text{smp}$ proportional to the residual phase error, sent to the GM.

Time-domain model

The CKFB edge samples a voltage ramp of slope $K_\text{slope}$; the sampled value is held until the next cycle, i.e. a zero-order hold. A phase error $\varphi$ at the reference is a time error $\varphi/(2\pi f_\text{ref})$, which the slope turns into a voltage $K_\text{slope}\,\varphi/(2\pi f_\text{ref})$. Hence the gain slide p20:

$$ K_\text{SPD} = \frac{K_\text{slope}}{2\pi\,f_\text{ref}} $$
With $K_\text{slope}=6$ GV/s and $f_\text{ref}=104$ MHz: $K_\text{slope}/f_\text{ref}=57.7$, so $K_\text{SPD}=57.7/(2\pi)=\mathbf{9.19\ V/rad}$ (the slide writes "$57/2\pi$") slide p20 derived. This is far larger than the effective $K_\text{PD}$ of a PFD-charge-pump, which is precisely why GM/comparator noise is suppressed.

Frequency-domain model

Because $V_\text{smp}$ is a ZOH updated once per reference cycle, the SPD behaves as a first-order IIR filter inside the loop slide p20. For $f\ll f_\text{ref}/2$ we approximate the sampled path by its continuous-time equivalent $K_\text{SPD}\cdot \tfrac{1}{1+s/\omega_\text{IIR}}$, with $\omega_\text{IIR}$ near $f_\text{ref}\times$(loop gain); a fully rigorous treatment is in the z-domain derived textbook (sampled-data PLL). The input-referred SPD+GM noise is white and enters at the reference, so it is low-pass shaped by $H_\text{ref}$.

Non-idealities ÔåÆ jitter mechanism

  • Comparator/GM offset: a static offset shifts the mean of the 1-bit error $e[k]=\operatorname{sign}(\text{PHE})$ away from zero, which biases the sign-LMS calibrations slide p27. A 1-bit ╬öV-DAC on $V_\text{ref}$ nulls it (the offset cal) slide p28.
  • SPD+GM thermal floor: contributes the "SPD+GM" slice of the budget ÔÇö about 3.9% of total jitter variance, suppressed by the large $K_\text{SPD}$ slide p42.

(3) GM transconductor + loop filter

3. GM + type-II loop filter

Purpose

The transconductor (GM) converts the held SPD voltage into a current that charges the loop-filter capacitors, producing the VCO control voltage(s). With the dual-path VCO it drives both a proportional node $V_\text{ctrl,P}$ and an integral node $V_\text{ctrl,I}$ (slide 39) ÔÇö together a type-II loop (zero + pole) slide p39.

Inputs / outputs

  • In: $V_\text{smp}$ (relative to $V_\text{ref}$) from the SPD.
  • Out: $V_\text{ctrl,P}$ / $V_\text{ctrl,I}$ to the dual-core VCO.

Time-domain model

GM is a voltage-controlled current source $i = g_m\,(V_\text{smp}-V_\text{ref})$, integrated onto the loop capacitors. Note the GM can be ┬ÁA-level because its noise is suppressed by the high $K_\text{SPD}$ ahead of it slide p20.

Frequency-domain model

The loop filter sets $G_\text{ol}(s)$. For the type-II shape used in the model, with a zero $\omega_z$ (proportional path) for phase margin and a pole $\omega_p$ to clean HF ripple textbook (Gardner) derived:

$$ G_\text{ol}(s)=K_\text{loop}\,\frac{1+s/\omega_z}{s^{2}\,(1+s/\omega_p)},\qquad \text{PM}=180^\circ+\angle G_\text{ol}(j\omega_c),\quad \omega_c=\sqrt{\omega_z\omega_p}\ \text{(max PM)} $$

The two poles at the origin are the VCO integrator plus the loop integrator (the $V_\text{ctrl,I}$ path). With the design knobs $f_c=1.5$ MHz, PM $=60^\circ$ assumption [A1][A2], the model measures $f_c=1.501$ MHz derived. Loop-filter / PD-output-referred noise sees a band-pass $(K_\text{vco}/s)/(1+G_\text{ol})$ derived.

Non-idealities ÔåÆ jitter mechanism

  • GM thermal/flicker noise: injected after the SPD; it is divided down by $K_\text{SPD}$ when input-referred, so it is a small slice (bundled into the "SPD+GM" Ôëê 3.9% of the budget) slide p42.
  • Loop-filter resistor noise: band-pass shaped around $f_c$; folded into the type-II response above.
The deck gives the topology (dual-path type-II, slide 39) but not numeric $R_z$, $C_z$, $C_p$, $g_m$. The loop component values are chosen in docs/assumptions.md [A1][A2] to hit $f_c=1.5$ MHz and PM $60^\circ$; treat them as a representative design, not a slide fact.

(4) VCO (dual-core LC)

4. Voltage-Controlled Oscillator

Purpose

Generates the 6.72 GHz output. As a phase block it is a pure integrator: control voltage ÔåÆ output frequency ÔåÆ output phase. It is the dominant noise contributor at Ôëê51.8% of the jitter budget slide p42.

Inputs / outputs

  • In: $V_\text{ctrl,P}$ / $V_\text{ctrl,I}$.
  • Out: CKVCO at $f_\text{out}=6.72$ GHz, divided down by the MMD.

Time-domain / frequency-domain model

Frequency is $f=f_0+K_\text{VCO}\,V_\text{ctrl}$ (with $K_\text{VCO}$ in Hz/V); phase is the integral of frequency, so the small-signal phase transfer is an integrator derived textbook:

$$ \frac{\Phi_\text{vco}(s)}{V_\text{ctrl}(s)} = \frac{2\pi\,K_\text{VCO}}{s} = \frac{K_\text{vco}}{s}\quad(K_\text{vco}\text{ in rad/s/V}) $$

Its own (free-running) phase noise is the white-FM $-20$ dB/dec slope plus a $1/f^3$ flicker region. Through the loop it is high-pass filtered:

$$ H_\text{vco}(s)=\frac{1}{1+G_\text{ol}(s)}\quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{LF: suppressed }(\to 0),\ \ \text{HF: passes }(\to 1) $$

So the loop scrubs close-in VCO noise up to $f_c$ and lets far-out VCO noise through ÔÇö the classic reason a higher $f_c$ helps VCO noise but hurts reference-domain noise derived.

The free-running VCO level (ÔêÆ116.5 dBc/Hz @ 1 MHz, $1/f^3$ corner 150 kHz) is back-solved in assumptions.md [A13] to reproduce the slide-42 split (VCO Ôëê 51%); the slides give the percentage, not the raw curve assumption [A13].

Non-idealities ÔåÆ jitter mechanism

  • VCO duty-cycle error: if the duty cycle Ôëá 50%, the two VCO phases used for the ┬¢-range divider are not exactly $T_\text{vco}/2$ apart, $\Delta t_\text{err}=\Delta t-T_\text{vco}/2\neq0$. This injects a SEL_CKFB-correlated error that corrupts $K_\text{DTC}$ and re-creates a spur slide p35; the vco_dcc sign-LMS removes it (see block 5) slide p36.
  • VCO phase noise: the largest single slice of the budget (Ôëê51.8%) ÔÇö high-pass shaped, dominant beyond $f_c$.

(5) MMD divider + modified ╬ú╬ö (DSM)

5. Multi-Modulus Divider + modified DSM (┬¢-range)

Purpose

The MMD divides CKVCO by $/N$ or $/N{+}1$ under control of a modified MASH ╬ú╬ö-modulator to realize the fractional ratio. The "modified" part also drives SEL_CKFB to pick between two VCO phases, halving the residual quantization-error range the DTC must cover slide p34.

Inputs / outputs

  • In: CKVCO; the fractional control word (here $\text{FCW}'=\text{FCW}+\texttt{ckref\_dcc}$, slide 40).
  • Out: CKFB to the SPD; the divide sequence $N_\text{DIV}$; the phase select $\text{SEL\_CKFB}\,(\pm1)$; the accumulated quantization error $\Phi_\text{QE}[n]$ that the DTC cancels.

Time-domain model ÔÇö DSM quantization-error range

The MASH order sets how far CKFB wanders, i.e. how much DTC range is needed slide p6:

MASH orderQE range $\Phi_e$
MASH1$[-T_\text{vco}/2,\,+T_\text{vco}/2]$
MASH1-1$[-T_\text{vco},\,+T_\text{vco}]$
MASH1-1-1$[-2T_\text{vco},\,+2T_\text{vco}]$

The ┬¢-range trick: switching CKFB1 ÔåÆ CKFB2 (= CKFB1 delayed by ┬¢$T_\text{vco}$) via SEL_CKFB folds the QE into $\Phi_e\in[0,\,0.5\,T_\text{vco}]$ instead of $[-T_\text{vco}/2,+T_\text{vco}/2]$ ÔÇö half the range, hence (from block 1) lower noise, power, and INL slide p34.

Frequency-domain model ÔÇö DSM noise shaping

A MASH-$m$ modulator produces high-pass-shaped quantization noise at the divider input textbook (Riley, JSSC 1993):

$$ S_{Q,\text{DSM}}(f)=\frac{1}{12}\cdot\frac{1}{f_\text{ref}}\big[\,2\sin(\pi f/f_\text{ref})\,\big]^{2m} $$

Referred to output phase it is multiplied by $(2\pi)^2$ (cycles ÔåÆ rad) and then low-pass filtered by the loop ÔÇö the classic "DSM hump." But the DTC cancels the in-band accumulated part (slide 9), so the residual collapses to Ôëê0% of the budget (the model applies an ~80 dB cancellation factor) slide p42 derived.

The cancellation chain (LMS, slide 40)

Three sign-LMS integrators share the 1-bit error $e[k]=\operatorname{sign}(\text{PHE})$ slide p40 textbook (Widrow & Stearns):

K_DTC[k+1] = K_DTC[k] + ╬╝2┬Àe[k]┬À╬ªe[kÔêÆn]
DTC gain cal ÔÇö the adaptive weight is $K_\text{DTC}$; main DTC term $=K_\text{DTC}\cdot\Phi_e[n]$ slide p26-28
vco_dcc[k+1] = vco_dcc[k] + ╬╝1┬ÀSEL_CKFB[k]┬À(e[k]ÔêÆe[kÔêÆ1])
VCO duty-cycle cal ÔÇö correlating with $\text{SEL\_CKFB}(\pm1)$ isolates the alternating duty error; converges to $\Delta t_\text{err}/2$, added to the DTC code slide p36,40
ckref_dcc[k+1] = ckref_dcc[k] + ╬╝3┬Àeven_cycle[k]┬À(e[k]ÔêÆe[kÔêÆ1])
CKREF duty-cycle cal ÔÇö regressor alternates every reference cycle; injected as $\text{FCW}'=\text{FCW}+\texttt{ckref\_dcc}$ before the modified DSM slide p40
Vref_adj[k+1] = Vref_adj[k] + ╬╝_os┬Àe[k]
Offset cal ÔÇö a DC servo driving the mean of $e[k]$ to zero (so the sign-LMS loops are unbiased), via a ╬ö╬ú-dithered 1-bit ╬öV-DAC slide p28,40

Final code: $\text{DTC code}=\operatorname{quantize}\!\big(K_\text{DTC}\,\Phi_e[n]+\texttt{vco\_dcc}+\texttt{offset}\big)$, all converging in <30 ┬Ás even with 20 ps VCO duty error, 32 mV comparator offset, and a 1 ns (57%) CKREF duty error injected slide p40.

Non-idealities ÔåÆ spur mechanism

  • Uncancelled DSM QE: would show as a fractional-spur hump; suppressed to Ôëê0 by the DTC + cals.
  • MMD input-referred noise: the divider's own thermal/flicker, the "MMD" slice Ôëê 6.2% of the budget ÔÇö distinct from DSM QN slide p42.
  • VCO / CKREF duty errors: create SEL_CKFB- / even-cycle-correlated tones; removed by vco_dcc / ckref_dcc above.
