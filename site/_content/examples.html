1 — DTC quantization noise floor

GIVEN

Time resolution slide p15 needs citation (target spec) $T_{res} = 400\ \text{fs}$; reference rate slide p42 $f_{ref} = 104\ \text{MHz}$. The DTC quantizes the reference edge once per reference cycle, so its timing error is white over the Nyquist band.

FORMULA slide p15 derived

A uniform time quantizer of step $T_{res}$ has timing variance $\sigma_t^2 = T_{res}^2/12$ textbook (Bennett quantization). Converting to reference phase via $\varphi = 2\pi f_{ref}\,\Delta t$ and spreading the variance over the full Nyquist span $f_{ref}$ gives the flat single-sided PSD:

$$\Phi_{\text{DTC,QN}} = \frac{(2\pi\,T_{res})^2}{12}\,f_{ref}\qquad[\text{rad}^2/\text{Hz}]$$

CALCULATION derived

$$\frac{(2\pi\cdot 400\times10^{-15})^2}{12}\cdot 104\times10^{6} = \frac{(2.513\times10^{-12})^2}{12}\cdot 1.04\times10^{8}$$ $$= \frac{6.317\times10^{-24}}{12}\cdot 1.04\times10^{8} = 5.264\times10^{-25}\cdot 1.04\times10^{8} = 5.47\times10^{-17}$$ $$\mathcal{L}_{\text{DTC,QN}} = 10\log_{10}(5.47\times10^{-17}) = -162.6\ \text{dBc/Hz}$$

INTERPRETATION

The 0.4-dB gap (−162.6 vs −163) is rounding in the slide. The PLL freq_domain.json carries dtc_qn_dbc = -163.0 as the modeled in-band DTC quantization floor. Because $\Phi_{\text{DTC,QN}}\propto T_{res}^2$, halving the LSB drops this floor by 6 dB — the motivation for the range-reduction tricks (slides 33/34/38).

±3 dB convention flag. Whether you normalize the variance by $f_{ref}$ (double-sided over $[-f_{ref}/2,\,f_{ref}/2]$) or by $f_{ref}/2$ (single-sided) is a factor-of-2 (3 dB) choice needs citation. We adopt the slide's $(2\pi T_{res})^2/12\cdot f_{ref}$ form because it is what reproduces −163 dBc/Hz; the single/double-sided ambiguity recurs throughout (see Example 5).

2 — DTC delay range

GIVEN slide p15

Resolution per LSB $T_{res} = 400\ \text{fs}$; DTC word width $n = 10\ \text{bits}$.

FORMULA slide p15 slide p33

$$DR = 2^{n}\cdot T_{res} = 2^{n}\cdot \ln 2\cdot R\cdot C_{LSB}$$

CALCULATION

$$DR = 2^{10}\cdot 400\times10^{-15} = 1024\cdot 4.0\times10^{-13} = 4.096\times10^{-10}\ \text{s}$$

INTERPRETATION

The DTC must span at least one reference-edge interval of fractional slip; ~400 ps with 10-bit resolution gives 400-fs LSBs. Since DTC power $\propto DR^2$ and DTC QN $\propto T_{res}^2$ slide p33, the design wants the smallest range that still covers the required edge motion — exactly why the ½-range (p34) and ⅛-range (p38) schemes exist.

3 — Sampling phase-detector gain $K_{SPD}$

GIVEN slide p20

Slope of the sampled waveform at the sampling instant $K_{slope} = 6\ \text{GV/s}$ needs citation (slide quotes "6 GV/s"); $f_{ref} = 104\ \text{MHz}$.

FORMULA slide p20

$$K_{SPD} = \frac{K_{slope}}{2\pi f_{ref}}\qquad[\text{V/rad}]$$

Reasoning: a phase error $\varphi$ rad at the reference equals a time error $\varphi/(2\pi f_{ref})$ s, which the slope converts to a sampled voltage $K_{slope}\cdot\varphi/(2\pi f_{ref}) = K_{SPD}\,\varphi$.

CALCULATION derived

$$\frac{K_{slope}}{f_{ref}} = \frac{6\times10^{9}}{104\times10^{6}} = 57.7\qquad\Rightarrow\qquad K_{SPD} = \frac{57.7}{2\pi} = 9.18\ \text{V/rad}$$

INTERPRETATION

The large $K_{SPD}\approx 9.2$ V/rad is the whole point of the sampling PD: it provides high "phase-to-voltage" gain so that the downstream GM/comparator noise, referred to the input phase, is divided by this gain and becomes negligible (it lands at only ~3.9% of the budget — see Example 5).

4 — Loop design: zero/pole from $f_c$ and PM

GIVEN assumption [A1][A2]

Crossover (loop bandwidth) $f_c = 1.5\ \text{MHz}$ [A1], phase margin $\text{PM} = 60^\circ$ [A2] (slide 42 gives no loop numbers). From freq_domain.json: $K_{vco}=100$ MHz/V, $N=64.62$, DSM order 2.

FORMULA textbook (Gardner, max-PM placement) derived

For a Type-II loop the PM is maximized when the crossover sits at the geometric mean of zero and pole, $\omega_c = \sqrt{\omega_z\,\omega_p}$. Defining the spacing factor $k = \omega_p/\omega_z$ in terms of PM:

$$k = \tan\!\left(45^\circ + \tfrac{\text{PM}}{2}\right) = \tan(75^\circ),\qquad \omega_z = \frac{\omega_c}{k},\quad \omega_p = \omega_c\,k$$

CALCULATION derived

$$k = \tan(75^\circ) = 3.732 \;\Rightarrow\; \omega_p/\omega_z = k^2 = 13.93$$ $$f_z = \frac{1.5\ \text{MHz}}{3.732} = 402\ \text{kHz},\qquad f_p = 1.5\ \text{MHz}\times 3.732 = 5.60\ \text{MHz}$$

5 — Jitter budget → integrated phase noise

GIVEN slide p42 model

$f_{out} = 6.72\ \text{GHz}$; total RMS jitter $\sigma_t = 87.6\ \text{fs}$ from the summed budget. Per-source contributions from freq_domain.json.

FORMULA textbook (IEEE-1139 / Leeson, small-angle) derived

$$\sigma_\varphi = 2\pi f_{out}\,\sigma_t,\qquad \text{IPN}_{\text{SSB}}(\text{dBc}) = 10\log_{10}\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\sigma_\varphi^2\right)$$

The $\tfrac{1}{2}$ is the single-sideband-from-double-sideband conversion (it removes 3 dB versus the DSB form $10\log_{10}\sigma_\varphi^2$).

CALCULATION derived

$$\sigma_\varphi = 2\pi\cdot 6.72\times10^{9}\cdot 87.6\times10^{-15} = 3.699\times10^{-3}\ \text{rad}$$ $$\sigma_\varphi^2 = 1.368\times10^{-5}\ \text{rad}^2$$ $$\text{IPN}_{\text{SSB}} = 10\log_{10}\!\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\cdot1.368\times10^{-5}\right) = -51.65\ \text{dBc}$$

(The DSB value would be $10\log_{10}(1.368\times10^{-5}) = -48.6$ dBc — the same 3-dB convention as Example 1.)

INTERPRETATION

The computed −51.65 dBc matches freq_domain.json's ipn_dbc_ssb = -51.65 and slide 42 (quoted −51.6 / −51.7) to within rounding. Sources add in RMS (variances sum): $\sigma_{tot}^2 = \sum_i \sigma_i^2$. The VCO dominates at ~52% because its phase noise is only high-pass-suppressed inside the loop bandwidth and passes unfiltered above $f_c$; REF + DTC together (~37.8%) are the next lever; the DSM quantization noise is nearly fully cancelled by the DTC (0.3%), which is the central claim of the architecture.

6 — Calibration convergence

GIVEN slide p40 — all four loops must settle in < 30 µs

Initial $K_{DTC}$ error assumption [see docs/assumptions.md] = 10% of a 1000-code nominal → 100 codes of error. Each loop uses a sign-sign-LMS update textbook (Widrow & Stearns):

$$w[k+1] = w[k] + \mu\cdot\operatorname{sign}(e[k])\cdot\operatorname{sign}(x[k])$$

FORMULA / MECHANISM slide p26-28,35-36,40 derived

For the DTC-gain loop, $E\{e\cdot\Phi_e\}\propto(g-\hat g)E\{\Phi_e^2\}$, giving a first-order recursion with time constant $\tau\approx 1/(\mu\,E\{\Phi_e^2\}\,2f_{ref}\,\eta)$, so the gain estimate decays exponentially to truth. The duty-cycle loops correlate $e[k]$ against a deterministic $\pm1$ regressor ($\texttt{SEL\_CKFB}$ for VCO duty, $\texttt{even\_cycle}$ for CKREF) to isolate the alternating error from random noise.

7 — Time-domain ↔ frequency-domain cross-check

GIVEN derived

Two independent models compute the same RMS jitter over the same band 1 kHz – 49.4 MHz ($\approx 0.95\cdot f_{ref}/2$, i.e. a 5% guard below the 52 MHz Nyquist edge, from time_domain.json):

  • Time-domain Monte-Carlo of the phase-error sequence → std of $\varphi[k]$.
  • Frequency-domain integral of the Welch PSD → $\sigma_t=\sqrt{\int S_\varphi\,df}/(2\pi f_{out})$.

FORMULA textbook (Welch 1967 / Parseval)

$$S_\varphi(f) = \frac{2}{f_s M U}\,\big|\text{FFT}(w\cdot\varphi)\big|^2,\qquad \int S_\varphi\,df \approx \operatorname{var}(\varphi)$$

The two estimators agree to < 0.05 dB and the Parseval variances match to ~5%, validating both the PSD normalization and the NTF construction. Self-check

Q1. The DTC quantization-noise floor is modeled as $-163$ dBc/Hz, yet the hand calculation in Example 1 returns a slightly different number. Starting from $\Phi_{\text{DTC,QN}} = (2\pi T_{res})^2/12 \cdot f_{ref}$ with $T_{res}=400$ fs and $f_{ref}=104$ MHz, what value do you compute, and why is it not exactly $-163$?
Show answer
$\Phi_{\text{DTC,QN}} = (2\pi\cdot400\times10^{-15})^2/12 \cdot 104\times10^{6} = 5.47\times10^{-17}\ \text{rad}^2/\text{Hz}$, so $\mathcal{L} = 10\log_{10}(5.47\times10^{-17}) = -162.6$ dBc/Hz derived. The 0.4-dB gap versus the $-163$ dBc/Hz carried in freq_domain.json / slide p15 is just slide rounding. Because $\Phi_{\text{DTC,QN}}\propto T_{res}^2$, halving the LSB lowers this floor by 6 dB.
Q2. The sampling phase detector quotes a slope $K_{slope}=6$ GV/s and $f_{ref}=104$ MHz. Using $K_{SPD}=K_{slope}/(2\pi f_{ref})$, what is $K_{SPD}$ in V/rad, and why is such a large gain desirable?
Show answer
$K_{slope}/f_{ref} = 6\times10^{9}/104\times10^{6} = 57.7$, hence $K_{SPD} = 57.7/(2\pi) = 9.18$ V/rad derived (matching the $\approx 9.18$ V/rad anchor; slide p20 quotes the rounded "$57/2\pi$"). The high phase-to-voltage gain divides down the downstream GM/comparator noise referred to the input phase, so SPD+GM lands at only $\approx 3.9\%$ of the jitter budget.
Q3. For the Type-II loop with $f_c=1.5$ MHz and $\text{PM}=60^\circ$, the spacing factor is $k=\tan(45^\circ+\text{PM}/2)$ and $f_z=f_c/k$, $f_p=f_c\,k$. Compute $k$, $f_z$, $f_p$. Then, given $\sigma_t=87.6$ fs at $f_{out}=6.72$ GHz, use $\text{IPN}_{\text{SSB}}=10\log_{10}(\tfrac{1}{2}\sigma_\varphi^2)$ with $\sigma_\varphi=2\pi f_{out}\sigma_t$ to find the integrated phase noise.
Show answer
$k=\tan(75^\circ)=3.732$, so $f_z=1.5\ \text{MHz}/3.732=402$ kHz and $f_p=1.5\ \text{MHz}\times3.732=5.60$ MHz derived (the max-PM geometric-mean placement $\omega_c=\sqrt{\omega_z\omega_p}$ that yields $60^\circ$ at crossover). For the IPN: $\sigma_\varphi=2\pi\cdot6.72\times10^{9}\cdot87.6\times10^{-15}=3.699\times10^{-3}$ rad, $\sigma_\varphi^2=1.368\times10^{-5}\ \text{rad}^2$, so $\text{IPN}_{\text{SSB}}=10\log_{10}(\tfrac{1}{2}\cdot1.368\times10^{-5})=-51.65$ dBc derived, matching freq_domain.json and slide p42.
