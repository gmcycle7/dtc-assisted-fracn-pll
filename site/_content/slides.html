Scope

This study covers slides 1 → 42 — the architecture, DTC circuit/noise, calibration, and noise-budget material. The deck is a tutorial, so it mixes (a) general PLL background, (b) a literature survey of DTC techniques from many groups, and (c) the author's own line of work (W. Wu, JSSC 2019 / ISSCC 2021 / RFIC 2023). The proposed architecture that the models implement is the DTC-assisted analog sampling fractional-N PLL with a stack of background digital calibrations, fully assembled on PDF p.39–40 slide p.39-40.

The 4 proposed calibrations

All background sign-LMS loops — but note the variant: the K_DTC gain cal is sign-error LMS (1-bit error × full-precision Φe), while the vco_dcc and ckref_dcc duty cals are sign-sign LMS (±1 regressor):

  1. DTC gain (K_DTC) — p.26–28, p.39–40
  2. CKREF duty-cycle (ckref_dcc) — p.39–40
  3. VCO duty-cycle (vco_dcc) — p.35–37, p.39–40
  4. Comparator/GM offset (PHE zero-mean) via ΔV-DAC on Vref_adj — p.27–28, p.39–40

Two range-reduction techniques

These make the DTC linear enough by shrinking its dynamic range:

  • ½-range using two VCO phases — p.34 (proposed)
  • 1/8-range using 8 RO phases — p.38 (survey, [C. Hwang ISSCC 2022])

Detailed notes on the key slides

These are the technically rich slides where the equations, models, and calibrations actually live. Each summarizes the per-slide deep notes from the inventory and shows the slide image.

p.5 / slide 5 — Linear PLL phase-domain model (the frequency-domain backbone)

The canonical phase-domain block diagram: $|\Phi_{ref}| \rightarrow (\Sigma) \rightarrow K_{PD}(s) \rightarrow H_{LF}(s) \rightarrow K_{VCO}/s \rightarrow |\Phi_{out}|$, with feedback $\div N$ returning $\Phi_{div,n}$. Noise injections are drawn at the summing node ($\Phi_{ref,n}$, $\Phi_{PD,n}$, $\Phi_{VCO,n}$, $\Phi_{div,n}$) plus the DSM $\Delta\Sigma$ quantization error $QE$ into the MMDIV. This is the type-II charge-pump-style textbook (Gardner / Razavi linear PLL) model and is the basis of models/frequency_domain_model.py. The bar example (SSB PN at a low-jitter 6-GHz PLL) shows VCO 51%, REF 39%, PD 4%, DIV 6% — VCO and REF dominate slide p.5.

p.9 / slide 9 — DTC-assisted PD principle

The DTC sits in the reference path and adds a programmable delay $\Delta t[n] = K_{DTC}^{-1} \cdot \Phi_{QE}[n]$ — a staircase that tracks the accumulated DSM quantization error $\Phi_{QE}$. Because the divider's CKFB edge in a frac-N PLL wanders by up to $\pm(\text{order})\cdot T_{vco}/2$ relative to CKREF, delaying CKREF by exactly that amount makes the PD see ≈0 phase error after lock — just like integer-N. Key relation: $K_{DTC} = T_{vco}/\Delta t_{DTC}$ (codes per VCO period). This enables a high-gain, small-dynamic-range PD → lower inband noise, better linearity, lower power slide p.9.

p.15 / slide 15 — DTC quantization noise

Verbatim formulas: the unit delay is $T_{res} = \ln 2 \cdot R \cdot C_{LSB}$, and the input-referred (at CKREF) phase-noise PSD is

$$\Phi_{DTC,QN} = \frac{(2\pi \cdot T_{res})^2}{12} \cdot f_{ref}.$$

Worked number: $T_{res} = 400$ fs, $f_{ref} = 104$ MHz $\Rightarrow$ −163 dBc/Hz (reproduced numerically in derivations.md, matching the $/12$ convention derived). A 10-bit DTC gives total DR = 400 ps, supporting a 2nd-order DSM with $f_{vco} > 5$ GHz slide p.15.

p.16 / slide 16 — DTC thermal noise

Starting from the slew-rate-referred jitter,

$$\mathcal{L} \cong 10\log_{10}\!\left[\tfrac{1}{2}\left(\frac{2\pi f_{ref}}{k_{slew}}\right)^2 S_v^{folded}(f_m)\right],$$

with $k_{slew} = 1/(2RC)$ evaluated at $VDD/2$ and $S_v^{folded}(f_m) \cong (kT/C)/(f_{out}/2)$. At mid-code this simplifies to

$$\mathcal{L} \cong 10\log_{10}\!\left[2kT \cdot f_{ref}\left(\frac{2\pi}{\ln 2}\right)^2 \frac{2^n T_{res}^2}{C_{LSB}}\right].$$

Needs $C_{LSB} \ge 2$ fF for a PN floor $< -171$ dBc/Hz; INV-buffer thermal noise dominates if the buffer width is too large slide p.16.

p.20 / slide 20 — Sampling PD (SPD) gain & dynamics

The sampled voltage $V_{smp}$ is a zero-order hold, so the SPD behaves as a 1st-order IIR filter inside the loop. Its gain is

$$K_{SPD} = \frac{K_{slope}}{2\pi \cdot f_{ref}}.$$

With $K_{slope} = 6$ GV/s and $f_{ref} = 104$ MHz this gives $K_{SPD} = 57/(2\pi) \approx 9.2$ V/rad $\gg K_{PD}$ of a PFD-CP. The GM stage can be µA-level because its noise is suppressed by the high $K_{SPD}$; with $C_{I,2} \approx 200$ fF the contribution is $< -170$ dBc/Hz slide p.20.

p.26–28 / slide 26–28 — DTC gain calibration (LMS)

System-identification view (p.26): the unknown "plant" is the mapping from accumulated QE $\Phi_e$ to the residual phase error; the adaptive weight $h(k)$ is $K_{DTC}$. The update is an LMS integrator $\mu \cdot Z^{-1}/(1 - Z^{-1})$:

$$K_{DTC}[k+1] = K_{DTC}[k] + \mu \cdot e[k] \cdot x[k], \quad x[k] = \Phi_e[k].$$

p.27 shows that comparator/GM offsets bias the mean of $e[k]$ (a sign-LMS converges only when $e[k]$ is zero-mean). p.28's fix: a 1-bit ΔV-DAC adjusts $V_{ref1}$ to null the offset, restoring zero-mean $\text{sign}(\text{PHE})$ slide p.26-28.

p.33 / slide 33 — DTC range-reduction tradeoffs (recap)

Pulls all the DR scaling laws together: $DR = 2^n \cdot t_{res} = 2^n \cdot \ln 2 \cdot R \cdot C_{LSB}$; quantization noise scales as $QN \propto t_{res}^2$; the thermal floor as $\mathcal{L} \cong 10\log_{10}[2kT \cdot f_{ref}(2\pi)^2/\ln 2 \cdot DR \cdot R]$; and power $\propto DR^2$. The takeaway: reducing DR improves noise, linearity, and power simultaneously, motivating the range-reduction techniques on p.34 and p.38 slide p.33.

p.34–37 / slide 34–37 — ½-range + VCO duty-cycle calibration

  • ½-range (p.34): a modified MMD + DSM selects between two VCO phases (CKFB1, CKFB2 = CKFB1 delayed by $\tfrac{1}{2}T_{vco}$) via $SEL\_CKFB$, halving the residual QE range the DTC must cover ($\Phi_e \in [0, 0.5\,T_{vco}]$ instead of $[-T_{vco}/2, +T_{vco}/2]$). Smaller DR ⇒ more linear, lower-noise, lower-power DTC.
  • Problem (p.35): if the VCO duty cycle $\neq 50\%$, the two phases are not exactly $\tfrac{1}{2}T_{vco}$ apart: $\Delta t_{err} = \Delta t - T_{vco}/2 \neq 0$. This injects a $SEL\_CKFB$-correlated error that corrupts the K_DTC cal and re-introduces a spur (ideal $\Delta t = T_{vco}/2 = 83$ ps @ 6 GHz).
  • Fix (p.36): a sign-LMS correlates $e[k]$ with $SEL\_CKFB\,(\pm 1)$ to estimate $\Delta t_{err}$, producing vco_dcc. $SEL\_CKFB = +1$ pushes CKDTC back by $\Delta t_{err}/2$; $= -1$ pulls in by $\Delta t_{err}/2$. Added into the DTC code.
  • Result (p.37): K_DTC converges $< 30$ µs even with 12% VCO duty error; the DTC code range is halved (FCAL 23 µs, FLL 91 µs) slide p.34-37.

p.39–40 / slide 39–40 — The complete proposed system + all calibrations

Signal chain (p.39): $\text{CKREF} (\times 2 \rightarrow \text{CKREFX2}) \rightarrow \text{DTC} \rightarrow \text{CKDTC} \rightarrow \text{SPD (slope-gen + sample)} \rightarrow V_1/V_2 \rightarrow \text{GM} (V_{ref}) \rightarrow V_{ctrl,P}/V_{ctrl,I} \rightarrow \text{dual-core VCO} \rightarrow \text{CKVCO} \rightarrow \text{MMD} (\div N) \rightarrow \text{CKFB}$, with the modified $\Delta\Sigma$-modulator (½-range) driving $NDIV$ and $SEL\_CKFB$ slide p.39.

Digital calibrations (p.40) — three sign-LMS integrators sharing the 1-bit error $e[k] = \text{sign}(\text{PHE})$:

  • $\mu_2$ branch (K_DTC): $K_{DTC}[k+1] = K_{DTC}[k] + \mu_2 \cdot e[k] \cdot \Phi_e[k-n]$. DTC code main term $= K_{DTC}\cdot\Phi_e[n]$.
  • $\mu_3$ branch (ckref_dcc): $even\_cycle\,(\pm 1)$ correlated with $(1-Z^{-1})e[k]$, integrated, multiplied by $even\_cycle$ → injected into the FCW path before the modified DSM (corrects the alternating-cycle error from a non-50% reference duty cycle).
  • $\mu_1$ branch (vco_dcc): $\text{Sign}(SEL\_CKFB)\,(\pm 1)$ correlated with $(1-Z^{-1})e[k]$, integrated, multiplied by $\text{Sign}(SEL\_CKFB)$ → added to the DTC code (corrects VCO duty error).
  • offset / $V_{ref\_adj}$ branch: a $\Delta\Sigma$-controlled ΔV-DAC keeps $e[k]$ zero-mean (cancels comparator + GM offset).

Final: $\text{DTC code} = \text{quantize}(K_{DTC}\cdot\Phi_e[n] + vco\_dcc + offset)$ and $FCW' = FCW + ckref\_dcc$ into the modified DSM. Sim (p.40): all converge $< 30$ µs and track PVT for both integer and fractional channels, with 20 ps VCO duty error, 32 mV comparator offset, 1 ns (57%) CKREF duty error injected slide p.40.
