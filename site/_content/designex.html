Design Walkthrough

A capstone: instead of verifying the Samsung design, we synthesize a new one from a single requirement â€” "build a 6.72 GHz fractional-N PLL whose integrated jitter is under 80 fs rms." We walk strictly forward â€” spec → budget → loop bandwidth → DTC sizing → calibration → figure-of-merit â€” and at every step we label each quantity as GIVEN (handed to us), CHOOSE (a design decision we make) or CHECK (the inequality the choice must satisfy). By the end you will have turned one number (80 fs) into a complete, internally consistent paper design.

Step 1 — SPEC: turn 80 fs into a phase spec

1.1 GIVEN / CHOOSE / CHECK

  • GIVEN: $f_\text{out}=6.72$ GHz, $f_\text{ref}=104$ MHz slide p42, integration band 1 kHz–100 MHz [A16].
  • CHOOSE: the jitter target $\sigma_t^\star = 80$ fs rms [A21].
  • CHECK: the implied integrated phase error and EVM must be acceptable for the modem this LO feeds.

1.2 The conversion

Jitter and integrated phase noise are the same quantity in two units. From derivations Â§8 the bridge is

$$ \sigma_\varphi = 2\pi\, f_\text{out}\,\sigma_t, \qquad \text{IPN}_\text{deck} = 10\log_{10}\!\big(\tfrac12\,\sigma_\varphi^2\big), \qquad \text{EVM}_\text{rms} \approx \sigma_\varphi . $$

The first relation just says "phase = $2\pi$ × frequency × time"; the $\tfrac12$ in the IPN is the same single-sideband factor the deck uses to report −51.7 dBc at 87.5 fs slide p42; and the last relation â€” EVM equals the rms phase error in radians â€” comes from the System page derived/model.

Show the algebra $$\sigma_\varphi = 2\pi (6.72\times10^{9})(80\times10^{-15}) = 3.378\times10^{-3}\ \text{rad}.$$ $$\text{IPN}_\text{deck} = 10\log_{10}\!\Big(\tfrac12 (3.378\times10^{-3})^2\Big) = 10\log_{10}(5.70\times10^{-6}) = -52.4\ \text{dBc}.$$ $$\text{IPN}_\text{total} = 10\log_{10}\big(\sigma_\varphi^2\big) = -49.4\ \text{dBc}\quad(\text{3 dB higher, total-variance convention}).$$ $$\text{EVM}_\text{rms} \approx 3.378\times10^{-3}\ \text{rad} = 0.338\%.$$

Step 2 — BUDGET ALLOCATION: split 80 fs in quadrature

2.1 GIVEN / CHOOSE / CHECK

  • GIVEN: jitter from uncorrelated blocks adds in power, i.e. in quadrature: $\sigma_{t,\text{tot}}^2=\sum_i \sigma_{t,i}^2$ textbook.
  • CHOOSE: a per-block sub-budget that mirrors the slide-42 split but with margin: VCO 57, REF 40, DTC 28, MMD 20, SPD+GM 17 fs.
  • CHECK: $\sqrt{\sum_i \sigma_{t,i}^2}\le 80$ fs.

2.2 Why quadrature, and why these shares

The five dominant blocks inject independent noise, so their RMS jitters do not add linearly â€” they add as a root-sum-of-squares (RSS). The slide-42 budget is dominated by the VCO (≈52 %) with REF+DTC next (≈38 %) and the MMD and sampling PD/GM contributing the rest slide p42. We keep that shape â€” it reflects real silicon difficulty (the VCO is always the hard part) â€” and scale every term down so the RSS clears 80 fs with a little headroom.

Show the RSS arithmetic $$\sigma_{t,\text{tot}}^2 = 57^2+40^2+28^2+20^2+17^2 = 3249+1600+784+400+289 = 6322\ \text{fs}^2.$$ $$\sigma_{t,\text{tot}} = \sqrt{6322} = 79.5\ \text{fs} \;\le\; 80\ \text{fs}.\quad\checkmark$$ Note how forgiving quadrature is: the 17-fs SPD term contributes only $17^2/6322=4.6\%$ of the variance, i.e. it moves the total from 79.5 to $\sqrt{6322-289}=77.6$ fs if you deleted it entirely. Spend your effort on the VCO. Dropping the VCO from 57 to 50 fs alone takes the total to $\sqrt{6322-3249+2500}=74.6$ fs.
The lever lesson. Because variance is quadratic, the largest term dominates the gradient: a 1-fs improvement on the 57-fs VCO removes $\approx 2\cdot57=114$ fs² of variance, while a 1-fs improvement on the 17-fs SPD removes only $\approx34$ fs². derived This is why every microwatt of synthesis effort goes to the VCO first, the reference path second.

Step 3 — OPTIMAL LOOP BANDWIDTH: find the U-curve minimum

3.1 GIVEN / CHOOSE / CHECK

  • GIVEN: in-band sources (REF, DTC, MMD, SPD) are low-passed by the loop → their integrated jitter grows with bandwidth; the VCO is high-passed → its integrated jitter shrinks with bandwidth derivations Â§2.
  • CHOOSE: the loop bandwidth $f_c$ that minimises the total.
  • CHECK: the minimum total $\le 80$ fs, and $f_c$ leaves room for stability ($f_c\ll f_\text{ref}/2$).

3.2 The trade-off is literally a U

Push the bandwidth up and the loop tracks (and so reproduces) more of the reference, DTC and divider noise â€” those terms rise. Push it down and the loop stops suppressing the VCO at low offsets â€” that term rises. The total is a sum of a rising curve and a falling curve, so it has a single minimum. Slide 42 states this exact trade-off â€” "PLL BW is chosen to trade off REF vs VCO" slide p42 â€” but gives no number; we compute the optimum.

Drag the loop-bandwidth slider: the REF/DTC/MMD jitter (in-band) climbs to the right, the VCO jitter (out-of-band) climbs to the left, and the RSS traces the U. The widget recomputes the budget live from the same model that produced freq_domain.json model.

3.3 Where the minimum sits

For the design's noise floors the model puts the U-curve minimum at $f_c^\star\approx 2.1$ MHz, giving ≈85.6 fs with the deck's looser floors model (verified list). The deck deliberately sits at the more conservative $f_c=1.5$ MHz / 87.6 fs [A1] â€” slightly to the left of the true optimum, trading ≈2 fs of jitter for extra phase margin and spur suppression. For our tighter floors (Steps 2 and 4) we keep the bandwidth near the optimum:

  • CHOOSE $f_c = 2.0$ MHz design choice â€” at/near the U minimum, where REF/DTC and VCO contributions balance.
  • CHOOSE phase margin $\text{PM}=60^\circ$ [A2] â€” the textbook well-damped target (gives $\zeta\approx0.97$, gain-peaking 1.70 dB, $\sim25\%$ step overshoot, 1 % settle $\sim1.1\,\mu$s) model (verified list).
  • CHECK $f_c/f_\text{ref} = 2.0/104 \approx 1/52$. With $f_c\ll f_\text{ref}/2=52$ MHz the sampled-loop phase erosion is small (the ZOH half-sample lag is $\approx-\pi f/f_\text{ref}$, only $\approx-3.5^\circ$ at $f_c$) model, so the continuous-time design is valid. $\checkmark$
Lock time falls out for free. A type-II loop locks in roughly $1/f_c$ model; at $f_c=2.0$ MHz that is $\sim0.5\,\mu$s of loop settling, comfortably inside the <30 µs calibration budget of Step 5.

Step 4 — DTC SIZING: range, resolution, bit count

4.1 GIVEN / CHOOSE / CHECK

  • GIVEN: a MASH-$m$ modulator emits quantization error spanning $1/2/4\;T_\text{vco}$ for MASH-1 / 1-1 / 1-1-1 slide p6; the DTC must cover that span so it can cancel it (slide 9). $T_\text{vco}=1/6.72\,\text{GHz}=148.8$ ps derived.
  • CHOOSE: the DSM order $m$ → the DTC range $\text{DR}$; the DTC resolution $T_\text{res}$; hence the bit count $n=\log_2(\text{DR}/T_\text{res})$.
  • CHECK: the DTC quantization-noise floor (slide-15 formula) must sit below the in-band noise floor implied by the 40-fs REF/DTC sub-budget.

4.2 Range from the MASH order

We use $m=2$ (MASH-1-1) [A5] as the default: it is the lowest order whose shaped quantization noise the loop and DTC can fully handle, and its DTC range is modest. From slide 6 the quantization-error span is $2\,T_\text{vco}$:

$$\text{DR} = 2\,T_\text{vco} = 2\times148.8\ \text{ps} = 297.6\ \text{ps}. $$

4.3 Resolution from the QN floor

The DTC's own quantization noise has the slide-15 PSD

$$ \mathcal{L}_\text{DTC,QN} = 10\log_{10}\!\Big[\frac{(2\pi\,T_\text{res})^2}{12}\,f_\text{ref}\Big]. $$

The deck's nominal $T_\text{res}=400$ fs gives −162.6 dBc/Hz (≈ slide's −163) slide p15 derivations Â§3. That is fine for 87.6 fs, but our DTC sub-budget is tighter (28 fs), so we halve the resolution to push the floor down ≈6 dB:

4.4 Bit count, and a range-reduction check

Bits follow directly from range and resolution:

$$ n = \log_2\!\frac{\text{DR}}{T_\text{res}} = \log_2\!\frac{297.6\ \text{ps}}{200\ \text{fs}} = \log_2(1488) = 10.5 \;\Rightarrow\; n = 11\ \text{bits (full range)}. $$

That is one bit more than the deck's $n=10$ slide p15, and bits cost power and INL. The fix is the deck's own range reduction: DTC power scales as $\text{DR}^2$ and QN as $T_\text{res}^2$ derivations Â§5 slide p33, so if calibration can predict and cancel the coarse part of the DSM ramp, the DTC only has to span the residual. Halving the range (the slide-34 Â˝-range technique) recovers a 10-bit converter at the same 200-fs resolution:

$$ \text{DR}_{1/2} = \tfrac12\cdot297.6 = 148.8\ \text{ps} \;\Rightarrow\; n = \log_2\!\frac{148.8\ \text{ps}}{200\ \text{fs}} = \log_2(744) = 9.5 \;\Rightarrow\; n = 10\ \text{bits.}\;\checkmark $$

Step 5 — CALIBRATION STEP SIZES: hit the residual-gain floor in 30 µs

5.1 GIVEN / CHOOSE / CHECK

  • GIVEN: a residual DTC-gain error $\epsilon$ leaves an in-band floor $S=\epsilon^2(2\pi)^2 S_{Q\text{dsm}}|H_\text{ref}|^2$, which integrates to $\approx4.5$ fs at $\epsilon=10^{-4}$ and $\approx45$ fs at $\epsilon=10^{-3}$ model (verified list); convergence target <30 µs (≈3120 reference cycles) [A17] slide p40.
  • CHOOSE: the sign-LMS step sizes $\mu$.
  • CHECK: steady-state $\epsilon\lesssim2\times10^{-4}$ (so the cal residual stays under the 28-fs DTC sub-budget) and the time constant $\tau$ gives <30 µs convergence.

5.2 The two competing requirements

Sign-LMS has the classic speed/accuracy tension. Larger $\mu$ converges faster (time constant $\tau\propto1/\mu$) but leaves a larger steady-state ripple â€” the misadjustment, which is $\propto\mu$ derivations Â§7. We size $\mu$ from the slower of the two constraints.

  • Accuracy: we need $\epsilon\lesssim2\times10^{-4}$. By the verified-list scaling ($\epsilon=10^{-4}\to4.5$ fs), $\epsilon=2\times10^{-4}$ leaves $\approx9$ fs model â€” well under the 28-fs DTC line, so there is margin for INL/NLC residual on top.
  • Speed: 30 µs at $f_\text{ref}$ is $30\,\mu\text{s}\times104\,\text{MHz}=3120$ update cycles. A first-order recursion settles in $\sim5\tau$, so we need $\tau\lesssim 6\,\mu\text{s}\approx620$ cycles, i.e. a per-update decay of $\sim1/620$ [A17].

For the sign-error LMS K_DTC loop (cal A: 1-bit error × full-precision $\Phi_e$), the gain update is $\hat g[k{+}1]=\hat g[k]+\mu_2\,e[k]\,\Phi_e[k{-}n]$ with time constant

$$ \tau \approx \frac{1}{\mu_2\,\eta\,E\{\Phi_e^2\}\,2 f_\text{ref}}, \qquad \eta=\frac{\sqrt{2/\pi}}{\sigma}\ \text{(sign-LMS slope factor)}, $$ derivations Â§7.1Widrow & Stearns.

So we CHOOSE $\mu$ to land $\tau\approx6\,\mu$s (the speed bound), then verify the resulting misadjustment keeps $\epsilon\lesssim2\times10^{-4}$ (the accuracy bound). When speed forces a larger $\mu$ than accuracy allows, the standard fix is gear-shifting: start with a large $\mu$ for fast acquisition, then drop to a small $\mu$ for a quiet steady state â€” the same idea as the deck's "converge <30 µs, then track" background scheme slide p40.

Show the cal-residual floor arithmetic

The verified residual-gain floor is linear in $\epsilon$ (the floor PSD $\propto\epsilon^2$, so integrated jitter $\propto\epsilon$):

$$\epsilon=10^{-4}\to4.5\text{ fs},\quad \epsilon=2\times10^{-4}\to 4.5\cdot2=9.0\text{ fs},\quad \epsilon=10^{-3}\to45\text{ fs},\quad \epsilon=10^{-2}\to449\text{ fs}.$$ To stay under the 28-fs DTC sub-budget with room for INL: pick $\epsilon\lesssim2\times10^{-4}$. That leaves $\sqrt{28^2-9^2}\approx26.5$ fs for residual nonlinearity (NLC).
Orthogonality keeps the loops from fighting. The K_DTC, vco_dcc, ckref_dcc and offset regressors ($\Phi_e$, SEL_CKFB, even_cycle, constant-1) are mutually orthogonal in expectation, so the four cal loops co-adapt independently model â€” except half-range $\Phi_e$ has a nonzero mean that couples the offset into the gain loop, which is why offset cal must converge first. For the polynomial NLC, use Legendre $P_1,P_2,P_3$ regressors (orthogonal on $[-1,1]$) rather than the correlated monomials $[D,D^2,D^3]$ for clean convergence model slide p31.

6.1 GIVEN / CHOOSE / CHECK

  • GIVEN: the jitter FoM $\text{FoM}_\text{jitter}=10\log_{10}\!\big[(\sigma_t)^2(P/1\text{mW})\big]$ derivations Â§8; the deck's power breakdown 14.2 mW (VCO 12 + DTC 1.2 + MMD 0.5 + rest 0.5) slide p39.
  • CHOOSE: reuse the slide-39 power envelope (our finer DTC adds little; range reduction keeps DTC power in check) → $P\approx14.2$ mW.

$$ \text{FoM}_\text{jitter} = 10\log_{10}\!\Big[(79.5\times10^{-15})^2\cdot 14.2\Big] = 10\log_{10}(8.98\times10^{-26}) = -250.5\ \text{dB}. $$

Q1. Your VCO comes in 6 fs worse than budgeted (63 instead of 57 fs). Re-RSS the total â€” does the design still pass 80 fs, and which single block would you tighten to recover?
Show answer
New variance $=63^2+40^2+28^2+20^2+17^2 = 3969+1600+784+400+289 = 7042$ fs², $\sqrt{7042}=83.9$ fs â€” fails by 3.9 fs. Because variance is quadratic, attack the next-largest term: tightening REF from 40 to 30 fs gives $3969+900+784+400+289 = 6342 \to \sqrt{6342}=79.6$ fs, back under spec. Tightening the 17-fs SPD to 0 would only reach $\sqrt{7042-289}=82.2$ fs â€” not enough, because small terms barely move the RSS. The lever is always the largest term.
Q2. Why did we keep $f_c$ near 2.0 MHz instead of pushing it to 5 MHz to "lock faster"?
Show answer
Two reasons. (1) Noise: above the U-curve minimum (≈2.1 MHz) the in-band REF/DTC/MMD jitter rises faster than the VCO jitter falls, so the total increases â€” a wider loop is worse for jitter here. (2) Stability/sampling: at 5 MHz, $f_c/f_\text{ref}=5/104\approx1/21$, and the ZOH half-sample lag $\approx-\pi f/f_\text{ref}\approx-8.6^\circ$ starts eroding phase margin. Lock time ($\sim1/f_c$) is already $\sim0.5\,\mu$s at 2 MHz â€” far inside the 30-µs cal budget â€” so there is nothing to gain and jitter/margin to lose. derived
Q3. The bit count came out to 11 at full range but we shipped 10. What two slide techniques made that possible, and what is the cost?
Show answer
We needed $n=\log_2(\text{DR}/T_\text{res})=\log_2(297.6\text{ ps}/200\text{ fs})=10.5\to11$ bits. Range reduction (slide-34 Â˝-range) halves DR to 148.8 ps so $n=\log_2(744)=9.5\to10$ bits at the same 200-fs resolution, and DTC power ($\propto\text{DR}^2$) drops 4×. The enabling technique is calibration: the cal loop must predict and cancel the coarse part of the DSM ramp so the DTC only spans the residual half-range. The cost is dependence on cal convergence/accuracy â€” if cal fails, the DTC can no longer cover the full DSM excursion. slide p33,p34
What this walkthrough does not prove. It is a paper synthesis: the noise floors behind the sub-budgets (Step 2) are the slide-42-anchored assumptions [A12]–[A15], the 80-fs spec is our input [A21], and the U-curve / loop numbers are from the LTI model model, not measured silicon. The deck's measured spurs (−70…−85 dBc slide p41) and PN are the real proof points; treat the FoM gain here as "what the formulas say a tighter VCO + finer DTC + near-optimal BW would buy," to be confirmed in extraction and silicon.
