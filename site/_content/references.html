References, Assumptions & Open Questions

Every number on this site traces back to one of four buckets — the deck itself, papers cited on slides 1–42, standard textbook theory, or items flagged as needing an external citation — and everything the slides do not state is collected here as explicit assumptions and open questions so it is never confused with a slide fact.

Citation policy. Citations are split into four buckets exactly as required, and no citation is invented. Where a result is standard but a specific page/source cannot be pinned, it is marked needs citation so a source can be supplied later. Anything the slide does give is used as-is and tagged to its slide page elsewhere on the site. Source: docs/references.md.

3. Standard PLL / CDR / DSP theory used in the models

These textbooks and classic papers supply the standard results behind the models. Anything tagged textbook on this site names its source from this list.

KeySourceWhat it provides

[Std-Bennett] W. R. Bennett, "Spectra of quantized signals," Bell Syst. Tech. J., 1948. Quantization variance $\Delta^2/12$ for the DTC/DSM quantizers.

Assumptions (full list)

Each assumption below is not stated in the slides (or not stated with a number) but is needed to build runnable models. Each has a rationale and a tag [A#]; on the site these appear as assumption with the matching [A#]. Source: docs/assumptions.md.

Reading guide. Items marked "on-slide" inside the rationale (e.g. [A6], [A9], [A14]) record numbers that are printed on the slides — they are listed here only because they anchor the model; they are not free assumptions.

Loop / system parameters

TagAssumptionRationale
[A1] Loop bandwidth $f_c\approx 1.5$ MHz. Slide 42 says "PLL BW is chosen to trade off REF vs VCO" but gives no number. For $f_{ref}=104$ MHz, $f_{out}=6.72$ GHz, and the VCO/REF crossover visible in the slide-42 PN plot (~1–2 MHz), 1.5 MHz is the standard choice ($\mathrm{BW}\approx f_{ref}/70$). Used for all frequency-domain plots. p42
[A2] Phase margin $\approx 60^\circ$. Not given. $60^\circ$ is the textbook target for a well-damped type-II PLL (≈ critically damped, minimal peaking). Sets the loop-filter zero/pole spacing.
[A3] Divide ratio $N\approx 64.6$. $=6720\,\mathrm{MHz}/104\,\mathrm{MHz}$, computed from slide-42 numbers; the fractional part exercises the DSM/DTC. p42
[A4] VCO gain $K_{VCO}=2\pi\cdot 100$ MHz/V. Typical for a 6-GHz LC-VCO with a switched-cap bank (coarse) and a small varactor (loop). Only the product with the loop filter sets BW, so this is a representative split.
[A5] DSM order $m=2$ (MASH or 2nd-order). Slide 15 says "can support 2nd-order DSM"; slide 6 lists MASH1 / MASH1-1 / MASH1-1-1. Default $m=2$ (MASH1-1), exposed as a parameter. p6, 15

DTC parameters

TagAssumptionRationale
[A6] DTC resolution $T_{res}=400$ fs, $n=10$ bits, $\mathrm{DR}=400$ ps. Directly from slide 15 (these are on the slide); used as the nominal design point. p15
[A7] $R=5\,\mathrm{k}\Omega$, $C_{LSB}=115$ aF per LSB so that $T_{res}=\ln2\cdot R\,C_{LSB}=400$ fs. Open questions / ambiguities

These are points where the slides are genuinely ambiguous and where guessing silently was avoided. Each states what was assumed (if anything) so it can be corrected against the source papers or measured chip. Source: docs/open_questions.md.

1. Type-I vs type-II loop. Slide 39 shows both a $V_{ctrl,P}$ and a $V_{ctrl,I}$ path (proportional + integral) into the VCO, suggesting a type-II sampling PLL, but slide 20 describes the SPD path as a 1st-order IIR (type-I-like). The model is a type-II loop (programmable proportional path) with a type-I switch exposed. Confirm which the measured chip used. p20, 39
2. Exact $\Phi_{DTC,QN}$ convention (slide 15). The printed $(2\pi T_{res})^2/12\cdot f_{ref}$ reproduces $-163$ dBc/Hz only with the $/12$ (double-sided-ish) reading; the textbook single-sided result has $/6$ → $\pm3$ dB. Confirm against [Wu'19]. (Also in references §4.) p15
3. $K_{DTC}$ numeric units in the convergence plots (slide 35/37). The plot shows $K_{DTC}\approx900$–$1013$. If $K_{DTC}=T_{vco}/\Delta t_{DTC}$, then at 6 GHz ($T_{vco}=166.7$ ps) a value of 1000 implies an effective LSB of ~167 fs (not the 400 fs of slide 15). The assumption is that 400 fs is the coarse DTC and the cal operates on an effective finer step; confirm the exact LSB used in the cal loop. p15, 35, 37
4. even_cycle vs SEL_CKFB regressors (slide 40). Both CKREF-DCC and VCO-DCC use a $\pm1$ "every-other-cycle" regressor. The assumption is that even_cycle toggles every reference cycle and SEL_CKFB toggles per the modified DSM phase-select pattern. Confirm the exact toggling. p40
5. Modified-DSM internals (slide 34). The "$\times2$ / $\div2$ / Acc / $Z^{-1}$" network that produces NDIV_tmp, SEL_CKFB, and the ½-range $\Phi_e(n)$ is only sketched. The model is a behavioral MASH1-1 that outputs $(\mathrm{NDIV},\,\mathrm{SEL\_CKFB},\,\Phi_e\in[0,0.5\,T_{vco}])$ consistent with the waveforms, but the exact register-level structure is not fully specified. p34
6. Reference doubler (CKREFX2, slide 39). Slide 39 shows $\mathrm{CKREF}\to\times2\to\mathrm{CKREFX2}\to\mathrm{DTC}$. Is the effective $f_{ref}$ for the cals 104 MHz or 208 MHz? The assumption is that the DTC/PD run at the doubled rate where shown but budget numbers are reported at 104 MHz (slide 42). Confirm. p39, 42
7. Absolute noise floors. Slide 42 gives only percentages of the 87.5-fs budget (VCO 51 %, REF 39 %, …), not absolute dBc/Hz per source (except DTC QN/thermal). The per-source PSD levels [A12]–[A15] are back-solved to reproduce those percentages; they are plausible but not unique. With the per-source PN curves they can be locked exactly. p42
8. Spur mechanism magnitudes (slide 41). Measured fractional spurs are $-70$…$-85$ dBc. The parabolic-INL spur model [A8] reproduces the trend (worse without range reduction / VCO DCC) but the absolute spur level depends on the true INL profile, which slides 17/18 show only qualitatively. p17, 18, 41
9. FoMjitter constant (slide 4). The benchmarking FoM is referenced ([Gao'09]) but the formula is not on the slide. The standard $\mathrm{FoM}=10\log_{10}\!\big[(\sigma_t)^2\cdot(P/1\,\mathrm{mW})\big]$ is used but flagged. p4
